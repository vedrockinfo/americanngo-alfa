Why Pilot?

Analysis of the report

  • The average mathematics score for 13-year-old students dropped by 5% between 2012 and 2020.
  • From 2020 to 2023, post-COVID, the situation worsened significantly with scores dropping from 280 to 271.
  • Average scores are reported on the NAEP LTT mathematics scale, which ranges from 0 to 500.

Analysis of the report

  • NAEP scores are reported at five key percentiles to track lower- (10th and 25th percentiles), middle- (50th percentile), and higher- (75th and 90th percentiles) performing students.
  • Since 2012, scores have significantly declined across all percentiles, except for the 90th percentile which remained stable at 239 from 2012 to 2020.
  • Post-COVID, the 2023 data shows a further significant decline in performance across all percentiles.
